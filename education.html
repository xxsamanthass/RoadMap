Education
+If you have a complete or partial elementary school education:

Every child under Plyler vs. Doe describes that every child under federal law must have equal access to public education from grades K-8. Talk to your school district about enrolling your child.

+If you have a complete or partial middle school education:

Every child under Plyler vs. Doe describes that every child under federal law must have equal access to public education from grades K-8.

However, if you are much older and in need of a lower education, programs similar to those on Keystone School Online are great for people who never entered highschool.

+ If you have a complete or partial high school education:

Online courses similar to those on Bringham Young University can help with getting your high school diploma. If that does not seem like the best choice for you, getting a GED, General Education Development, can help with getting a job.

+ If you have a complete or partial college education:

High ranking colleges like that at Georgia Tech have affordable online courses for those wanting to get their Bachelor's or Master's

American University also has tuiton-free degrees. It is now called University of the People, offering students online classes at Yale University and New York University.

Thomas Reuters Foundation provides refugees with classes at Harvard University for as low as $29. Courses will be provided in a range of topics, such as computer coding and neuroscience.

+ Free Coding Programs:

Girls Who Code is a free summer immersion program that provides girls in high school free computer science skills. Girls learn languages such as Python, HTML, CSS, and JavaScript. Girls also learn how to program a Arduion robot

#BuiltbyGirls provides girls with mentorship programs at different companies, such as Spotify, Microsoft, Twitter, and Facebook. Girls have options to submit ideas and go to Silicon Valley and win up $10,000 for their tech ideas.
