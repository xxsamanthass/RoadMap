Frequently Asked Questions
+What happens if the family is seperated due to divorce or death upon arrival to the US?

If family is separated on arrival to US due to divorce or death, the dependent family will be able to remain in the country.

+ Is a minor without a family is still eligible for refugee status?

Yes.

+ What happens if I do not have a passport or any other type of identification?

If you do not have any type of identification, then you must contact your UNHCR office.

+ What can I do if I am a refugee already locarted in the US and I want to bring over family?

You must contact a UNHCR office to get in contact with your family or you can fill out form I-730, which is for Refugee Relative Petition.

+ How long does the overall process take?

It depends on how quickly your UNHCR office works. If you have a lot of red flags show up, it will take longer. However, on average, it takes around 3 months to process. After that all you must do is find a host family and travel to the U.S.

+ What do I do if I cannot speak English?

This website has a translation bar at the top right corner that allows you to choose your native language. In terms of applications, your UNHCR office can translate for you.

+ Where do refugees settle in the U.S.?

Texas, California, Michigan, Florida, and North Carolina have the largest refugee populations.
