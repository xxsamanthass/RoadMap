I am a Refugee

  • Step 1: Who to contact?
    • Contact a UNHCR (United Nations High Commissioner for Refugees) office or an U.S. embassy office near you
    • You will fill out an application starting with, which is classification for refugee status
  • Step 2: RSC Forms
    You will get referred to the RSC (Resettlement Support Center), where you will be asked to fill out form G-325C which is basic information
  • Step 3: Background Check and Interview
    Officers from the Department of Homeland Security U.S. Citizenship and Immigration Services(USCIS), FBI, National Counterterrorism Center, and State Department look at all the information that the RSC has collected and conduct an in-person before deciding whether to approve them for resettlement in the US.
  • Step 4: Identification and Proof
    You will also need to fill out form FD-258.
    • This form will ask for information including:
      • A form of identification like a passport, a driver's license and a student ID
      • Proof you were persecuted which can be from a personal sworn statement and friends, relatives, doctors, anyone in an official position, newspaper articles or reports by a human rights organization
      • Copies of any membership cards or documents from your group that you have
  • Step 5: Physical
    You must undergo a health screening to identity medical needs and to ensure that those with a contagious disease, such as tuberculosis, do not enter the United States
  • Step 6: Sponsorship
    You find a "sponsorship assurance" by the RSC from a U.S.-based resttlement agency that is experienced in providing assistance to newly arrived refugees. The sponsor will fill out form I-134
  • Step 7: Orientation
    You must undergo a brief U.S. cultural orientation course prior to departure to departure for the United States
  • Step 8: Arrival
    You recieve assistance upon arrival in the United States through the Department of State's Reception and Placement Program
    • You will complete form I-94 on arrival

    • After One Year

    • Step 1: Apply for a green card
      Qualifications:
      • Must be present in the U.S.
      • Have no criminal history in the U.S.
    • Step 2: Remaining forms
      Fill out form I-485 , hand in form I-94, two passport styled photographs, copy of your government-issued identity document with photograph, copy of your birth certificate (if available), copy of your passport page with admission or parole stamp (issued by a U.S. immigration officer) and form I-693 which is a report of yor medical examination and vaccination record
