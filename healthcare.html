Healthcare
+If you have a union job:

Most union jobs have healthcare with a low cost. However, if your job does not offer healthcare ... see below

+Affordable Care Act:

The Afforable Care Act allows adult refugees to have Medicaid coverage.

The Pre-Existing Condition Insurance Plan has criteria that if followed allows free healthcare.

+Language Barries:

A language barrier makes it difficult for refugees to ask for help. However, according to the 1964 Civil Rights Act, all hospitals must have interpeters in physical and mental health emergency rooms.

+Families with children under 21:

Temporary Assistance for Needy Families have 100% government supported programs that provide refugee families with free healthcare.

Refugee Cash Assistance (RCA) and Refugee Medical Assistance (RMA) is another government funded program that provides refugees eight months until they must find healthcare. For the first eight months, however, they provide refugees with completely free healthcare and even help them find new healthcare.

+ Additional Resources:
